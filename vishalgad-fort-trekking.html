Vishalgad Fort Trekking : Rich History and Natural Beauty

  • Travel, Lifestyle, Trekking, Vishalgad Fort
  • Wikipedia

Nestled in the Sahyadri Mountains of Maharashtra, Vishalgad is a hidden gem for trekkers and history enthusiasts alike. This picturesque fort, located near the town of Kolhapur, offers not only a challenging trek but also a glimpse into the rich history of the Maratha Empire. In this article, we will take you on a virtual journey to Vishalgad, providing you with all the essential details you need to plan your trek and make the most of your visit, including a peek into the fort's fascinating history.

Trek Difficulty: Moderate

Best Time to Visit: October to March

How to Reach Vishalgad

By Road:
From Kolhapur:

Vishalgad is approximately 75 kilometers away from Kolhapur. You can hire a taxi or drive yourself to the base village of Vishalgad, which is Bhuibawada.

The journey takes around 2-3 hours, offering scenic views of the Western Ghats.

From Pune:

If you're traveling from Pune, Vishalgad is approximately 240 kilometers away. You can take a bus or drive to Kolhapur and then proceed to Bhuibawada.

By Train:
From Mumbai or Pune:

The nearest major railway station is Kolhapur Railway Station. From there, you can hire a taxi or take a bus to reach Bhuibawada.

Base Village to Samrad Village:

Start your trek from Bhuibawada and head towards Samrad village, which is the base for the trek.

The trail takes you through lush green fields, small streams, and dense forests. It's a scenic 6-kilometer walk to Samrad.

Samrad Village to Vishalgad:

From Samrad, trek to the Vishalgad fort. The trail is approximately 8 kilometers long and gradually ascends through rocky terrain.

Along the way, you'll encounter a few waterfalls during the monsoon season, adding to the charm of the trek.

As you approach the fort, the panoramic views of the Sahyadri range will leave you spellbound.

Exploring Vishalgad:

Once you reach the fort, take your time to explore its historical marvels. Vishalgad is known for its massive entrance, bastions, and a deep well called the "Nane Ghat."

A Glimpse into Vishalgad's History

Vishalgad has a storied history dating back to the 17th century. It was originally known as "Khelna" and was later renamed Vishalgad, meaning "Grand Fort," by the Maratha ruler Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj. The fort served as a strategic stronghold for the Marathas during their reign.

One of the most significant events in the fort's history was the daring escape of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj from the clutches of his rival, Siddi Johar, in 1660. Shivaji Maharaj and his trusted general, Tanaji Malusare, executed a brilliant plan to recapture the fort, which had been taken by the enemy. This event, known as the Battle of Pavan Khind, is a testament to the fort's historical importance.

FAQs about Vishalgad Trek

Is the trek suitable for beginners?

While the trek is considered moderate, it is doable for beginners with reasonable fitness levels. It's important to be prepared and carry essential trekking gear.

Are there any accommodations available on Vishalgad?

There are no accommodations on the fort itself. Trekkers usually opt for homestays in nearby villages or campsites near Samrad village.

Can I trek to Vishalgad during the monsoon season?

Monsoon brings lush greenery to the region and picturesque waterfalls, but the trail can be slippery and challenging. It's recommended for experienced trekkers.

Are there any nearby attractions?
Yes, you can explore the famous Amba Ghat and Amba-Awale Wildlife Sanctuary, which are not far from Vishalgad.

What should I carry for the trek?

Essentials include comfortable trekking shoes, sufficient water, snacks, a first-aid kit, a raincoat (during monsoon), and a good backpack.
